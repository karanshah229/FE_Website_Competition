ANGELINA JOLIE
About
Angelina Jolie is an American actress, filmmaker, and humanitarian. She has received an Academy Award, two Screen Actors Guild Awards, and three Golden Globe Awards, and has been cited as Hollywood's highest-paid actress. Jolie made her screen debut as a child alongside her father, Jon Voight, in Lookin' to Get Out (1982). Her film career began in earnest a decade later with the low-budget production Cyborg 2 (1993), followed by her first leading role in a major film, Hackers (1995). She starred in the critically acclaimed biographical cable films George Wallace (1997) and Gia (1998), and won an Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress for her performance in the drama Girl, Interrupted (1999).
Fight Back!
Children, I need you. We all need you. We adults... We are a little lost these days. We want you to think that we have it all under control, that it will all be fine. And it will be. But not because we know better or just because we love you. It's because we are betting on you. The world has changed so much in the last fifty years. In many ways it's a world you will understand better how to live in far better than we do. The internet and mobile phones... They were not around when I was your age. You can go home tonight and you can play games with kids your age in other countries. You can ask them about their lives. You can use your phone to translate other language. You have the world literally at your fingertips. But what will you do with it? This means that you can grow up to be citizens who together can complete the work that my generation will leave unfinshed and fight for universal human rights for all children.
